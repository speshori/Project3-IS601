How the Internet Works



IP Addresses

IP addresses are 32-bit numbers that uniquely identify a host, a computer or device, on a network. They are usually expressed in dotted decimal form and can also be represented in binary. There are four groups of numbers of 3 digits each which hold the decimal form of the IP address, making up 12 digits. An IP address has two parts, the network portion of the address and the host portion. The IP address will also have a subnet mask which will identify whether a host is on a local or remote subnet.
